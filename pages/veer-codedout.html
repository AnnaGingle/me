Alleviating pain points in the voter experience

kin+carta in partnership with columbia college chicago. 

Beginning project discussions were focused on large gaps and disparities between different districts of America for voters. We had to look into voting as a concept before we broke it down into physical voting practices and had to acknowledge the clear sensitivity around voting Americans possess.
Final Direction: Due to the intricate nature of voting and the short time frame our team was given, we elected our final deliverable to be a thoroughly researched case study. This will be handed off to an internal Columbia College team to fully develop Veer based off of our 39-page case study and a high-fidelity prototype.

TurboTax

TurboTax was helpful in analyzing already existing conversational interfaces out in the market. It is already used by Americans dealing with government taxes and other financial problems. This link between a government issue and a brand making an interface that has ease of use is something we sought out to replicate. They have already tackled the problem of writing legal jargon in a capacity for the average user to understand. We pulled both interactive design and content writing as inspiration for Veer.
